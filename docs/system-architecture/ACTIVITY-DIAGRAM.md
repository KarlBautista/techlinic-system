# Activity Diagram — TechClinic System

The activity diagram presents the workflow and sequence of activities within the TechClinic: Health Record Management System. It illustrates the step-by-step processes performed by each user role — Student, Nurse, Doctor, and Admin — using four swimlanes to clearly delineate responsibilities and interactions across the system.

The workflow begins in the Student swimlane, where a student opens the landing page on the clinic kiosk and enters their patient information. A decision point determines whether the student is a returning visitor, in which case their data is auto-filled from previous records, or a new patient who must fill in the form manually. Upon submission, the system executes a parallel fork: one path creates the patient record in the database, while the other simultaneously sends a registration notification to the clinic staff. The student then receives a confirmation, concluding their interaction with the system.

In the Nurse swimlane, the nurse logs in to the system and views the dashboard to monitor clinic activity. A decision point allows the nurse to select from available actions. If the nurse chooses to create a new patient visit, they enter the patient data and submit the record for processing. Alternatively, the nurse may manage the medicine inventory, where a decision point evaluates whether any medicine is low in stock — if so, a stock alert is triggered to notify the appropriate staff. After completing the selected task, the nurse can view patient records and access reports and charts for clinic analytics before the workflow concludes.

The Doctor swimlane follows a clinical workflow path. After logging in and viewing the dashboard, the doctor selects an incomplete patient record that requires medical attention. The doctor then adds a diagnosis, including the disease, treatment plan, and prescribed medication, and marks the record status as complete. A decision point determines whether a medical certificate or prescription should be generated — if yes, the document is created for the patient. The doctor may then view analytics, medicine inventory, and notifications before adjusting settings and concluding the workflow.

In the Admin swimlane, the administrator logs in and accesses the admin dashboard for system oversight. A decision point presents the available administrative actions: managing user accounts (activating or deactivating staff accounts) or viewing activity logs and analytics reports. After completing the selected actions, the admin may also manage personnel records before the workflow concludes.

Together, the four swimlanes demonstrate how each user role contributes to the overall operation of the clinic management system. The diagram highlights the parallel and sequential nature of tasks, the decision points that guide user actions, and the cross-lane interactions — such as the registration notification flowing from the Student process to the Nurse lane. This representation ensures a comprehensive understanding of the system's operational workflow, supporting efficient healthcare service delivery and health record management at the university clinic.
